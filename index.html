Hello and welcome to this comprehensive guide on status effects available in Heroes of the Storm. This guide explains all available status effects and, to some extent, interactions between them or with other abilities and can be used not only as a guide for the players who’ve just started their journey in the Nexus but also act as a refresher for more experienced travelers and bring back those sweet memories about clutch Zeratul core saves or E.T.C. + Sylvanas vs. Falstad confrontations.

Negative effects (debuffs)

In the first section we will review negative effects that can be applied to heroes or environment and explain “crowd control” through the prism of these effects so you can reason about picking heroes that counter other heroes.

Crowd-control (CC)

In Heroes of the Storm, most of the debuffs are “crowd-control” effects, meaning they influence heroes' ability to fight and/or move around the battlefield. When talking about crowd-control, the most notable effects of the debuffs are:

  • Impact on movement: debuff renders target immobile or decreases it’s movement speed
  • Impact on auto-attacks: debuff blocks or reduces target’s auto-attack (a.k.a. “basic attack”) damage.
  • Impact on abilities: debuff blocks the usage of abilities or interrupts “channeled” abilities, i.e. the abilities that have some duration and influence not only heroes that casted them but also the other heroes (this definition is not precise, though, as talents like Imposing Presence influence others but are not channeled). Also, some debuffs like stuns, Mind Control or Fear block the ability to hearthstone (teleport back to the Nexus Hall)
  • Impact on targeting: debuff blocks the ability to target heroes. This means your allies cannot damage the affected hero with basic attacks or abilities but the enemy’s support also cannot heal or buff them.

Stun

The most powerful and the most widespread form of CC that is available in forms of basic and heroic abilities, inferior only to death. Affected target cannot move or cast any abilities and channeled abilities are interrupted when the target that casted them is stunned. Because of its tremendous impact stuns are usually tied to skillshot abilities (meaning you have to aim the ability and hit enemy with it a.k.a. “land a skillshot”) or heroics; nevertheless, some heroes have them as targeted (point-and-click) basic abilities or AOE (area-of-effect) abilities (meaning the effect of the ability applies to all targets in the area the ability goes off in).

Every class in the game have heroes with stuns, even in their basic kit. Some examples include:

Silence

Silence is the second most powerful basic CC effect because a lot of heroes rely on their abilities to deal damage, escape death after a deep dive into the enemy lines or support their allies.
Because silence ticks less boxes than stun and almost always is a heroic ability (with the exception of Alarak’s Discord Strike basic ability), it is usually an AOE spell and has longer duration than stuns.

Silence effect in its pure form is available to these heroes:

Hero Class Ability Type
Alarak Assassin Discord Strike Basic
The Butcher Assassin Lamb To The Slaughter1 Heroic
Sylvanas Specialist Wailing Arrow Heroic
Malfurion Support Twilight Dream Heroic
  1. The Butcher’s ability at level 10 only applies to one hero; with level 20 upgrade, Slaughterhouse, it can silence all enemy heroes in the area. Both original and upgraded abilities also apply root-like effect.

Root

Root’s only effect is to stop target in place. Although less influential than stun, it can save your fleeing ally from their chasers or guarantee landing a powerful follow-up, e.g. AOE damage abilities.

Like stuns, roots are also available to a lot of heroes in form of basic abilities. For example:

Hero Class Ability Type
Arthas Warrior Howling Blast Basic
Thrall Assassin Feral Spirit Basic
Malfurion Support Entangling Roots Basic
Xul Specialist Bone Prison Basic

Because roots are less impactful than stuns or silences, not a lot of heroic abilities have root effects. One example here can be a level 20 upgrade, Kill Command, for Rexxar’s heroic ability Unleash the Boars.

Blind

Blind makes affected enemy heroes' auto-attacks miss their target; in other words, their auto-attacks deal no damage to your allies. This ability is a bane of auto-attack assassins like Illidan or Tracer, whose whole kit is built around dealing auto-attack damage.

Blind can be a basic ability, a talent upgrade or a heroic ability. Examples:

Hero Class Ability Type
Artanis Warrior Suppression Pulse Heroic
Johanna Warrior Shield Glare Basic
Auriel Support Blinding Flash Talent upgrade

Knockback and Displacement

Probably the most controversial form of crowd control and one of the hardest to apply effectively to an enemy because a lot of KBs are either skillshots or heroic abilities. The upside over roots, hovewer, is that KBs can interrupt channeled abilities.

As a recurring theme, knockbacks are available in every class. Examples:

Hero Class Ability Type
E.T.C. Warrior Face Melt Basic
Falstad Assassin Mighty Gust Heroic
Lt. Morales Support Displacement Grenade Basic
Sgt. Hammer Specialist Concussive Blast Basic

One peculiar thing about displacement is that while being an effect applied to an enemy, two heroes can also be “displaced” while using their displacement abilities:

  • Phase Prism makes Artanis switch places with an enemy hero
  • Alarak can use his Telekinesis on enemies as well as himself.

Polymorph

Polymorph is the combination of two basic negative status effects, namely Silence and Blind. Polymorphed enemies cannot auto-attack and use their abilities, but can move and be targeted with damage and heals.

As of November 2016, only two heroes have polymorphing ability in their kit:

Hero Class Ability Type Comment
Brightwing Support Polymorph Basic Also slows target’s movement speed while they’re polymorphed
Medivh Specialist Poly Bomb Heroic Recasts in the area after expiration; therefore, theoretically can be applied endlessly

Stasis

Stasis is quite widespread yet unique effect, both in targets it can be applied to and effects it causes on them. The following heroes have abilities that apply stasis-like effects on enemies:

Hero Class Ability Type
Anub'arak Warrior Cocoon Heroic
Arthas Warrior Summon Sindragosa Heroic
Stitches Warrior Gorge Heroic
Chromie Assassin Time Trap Basic
Zeratul Assassin Void Prison Heroic
Medivh Specialist Ley Line Seal Heroic
Sylvanas Specialist Black Arrows Trait
Zagara Specialist Devouring Maw Heroic

  • Arthas' Summon Sindragosa and Sylvanas' Black Arrows disable enemy’s minions, mercenaries and structures (except for Core and bosses). Affected targets stop firing at your allied heroes, minions, mercenaries or bosses.

  • Chromie’s Time Trap and Zeratul’s Void Prison stop time. Affected target’s cannot move, cast abilities or receive any damage or healing and their cooldowns stop refreshing during the duration of stasis. However, buffs and channeled abilities are not interrupted and continue after stasis effect wears off. Affected heroes are also targetable, so the stasis effect can be removed from them with Cleanse.

    Zeratul’s Void Prison also affects allies (if level 20 upgrade, Protective Prison, is not taken) and buildings which means in some cases you can cast VP on your allies or even your Core to block any damage targeted at them. However, Zeratul himself is not affected by his VP and can receive damage even when being inside the VP bubble.

  • Anub'arak’s Cocoon, Stitches' Gorge and Zagara’s Devouring Maw remove affected heroes from the game for a short duration. Affected heroes become untargetable (cannot receive damage or healing) and their channeled abilities are interrupted.

    Anub'arak’s Cocoon makes affected hero untargetable; however, their allies can attack cocoon to free the imprisoned hero.

    Zagara’s Devouring Maw can catch Unstoppable heroes.

Mind Control, Fear and Holy Ground

These CC effects are unique to certain heroes.

Mind Control is the effect of Sylvanas' Mind Control heroic ability. It allows Sylvanas' player to control movements of the affected enemy hero who also cannot cast abilities while being mind controlled. Mind Control also interrupts channeled abilities like E.T.C.’s Mosh Pit or Li Li's Jug of 1,000 Cups. However, Sylvanas cannot move stunned or otherwise immobile targets (like Sgt. Hammer in siege mode) with Mind Control.

Fear is the effect of Gul'dan’s Horrify heroic ability. Affected heroes cannot cast their abilities and their players lose control over their movement as feared heroes move “unpredictably” for the duration of Fear effect.

Holy Ground is the effect of the Tyrael’s level 16 talent Holy Ground. While not directly applied to enemy heroes, it creates an small impassable zone in the point to which Tyrael has teleported with his El'Druin’s Might basic ability.

Other debuffs

Movement and attack speed slow

These negative effects are, in fact, pure crowd control but they weren’t included in the table because they do not influence ability usage or targeting.

However, slows are very powerful form of CC and a lot of them are either passive or AOE abilities which make them easier to use than, say, roots or blinds. Movement speed slow makes it harder to retreat from the unfavourable fight and attack speed slow can severely decrease damage some auto-attack heroes can inflict on your allies.

Slows are available in all kinds of forms to any class. One notable hero here is Jaina whose trait, Frostbite, applies slow with every ability she lands on enemy.

Poison

Poison or damage-over-time (DOT) is the first non-CC debuff. Poisoned target receives periodical damage for the duration of poison. DOT effect also blocks Zeratul’s and Nova’s ability to slide back into stealth for the duration of poison. But unlike direct damage, DOT can be outhealed by support, well or Nexus Hall or removed by Cleanse or other abilities (like Tracer’s Recall).

A lot of heroes have abilities to apply poison to enemy targets but only Lunara with her trait, Nature Toxin, poisons her targets with any damage she deals.

Vulnerability

Vulnerability is the second negative non-CC status effect. Target affected by it receives increased damage for the duration of Vulnerability. As any other negative effect, it can be removed with Cleanse or abilities like Tracer’s Recall.

Vulnerability can be a part of the trait, talent upgrade, heroic ability or a part of the basic hero kit. Examples:

Hero Class Ability Type
Greymane Assassin Marked for the Kill Heroic
Jaina Assassin Frostbite Trait
Tyrande Support Hunter’s Mark Basic
Sylvanas Specialist Cold Embrace Talent upgrade

Vulnerability can be applied to heroes, minions, mercenaries, bosses and, in some cases, to structures (Hunter’s Mark being one of the exceptions).

Reveal

Some heroes can “reveal” (in other words, “make visible”) enemies with their abilities or talents. Reveal can bring enemies out of stealth or make them visible in bushes or behind object that breaks line of sight (like gates). This debuff can help you evade a bush ambush (cough “Chinese bush meta” cough) or auto-attack an enemy that fled behind the gate or bushes. Examples:

Hero Class Ability Type
Tracer Assassin Tracer Rounds Talent upgrade
Tassadar Support Oracle Trait

Positive effects (buffs)

Now that we are familiar with negative status effects, let’s continue with learning about effects that block application of or eliminate applied debuffs. Like in the previous section, there are several positive effects which buffs can grant:

  • Damage mitigation: buff can make target immune to all damage or some forms of it
  • CC mitigation: buff can make target to become unsusceptible to CC or remove applied CC effects
  • Targeting: buff can make hero untargetable, meaning they cannot receive auto-attack damage or targeted abilities damage but may (or may not, depending on the status effect) still be hit with AOE or skillshot. Same applies to healing and buffs the affected hero can receive
  • Movement/Auto-attack/Abilities: buff can block hero’s ability to move, auto-attack or use abilities

Unstoppable

Unstoppable is the most common buff which also has the most diverse interactions with debuffs. Unstoppable can be obtained through basic or heroic abilities from your own kit or received from another hero.

Cleanse

When received from another hero, Unstoppable removes all negative effects (bar some Stasis cases when hero is not targetable or stuck abilities like Tracer’s Pulse Bomb, Kael'thas’ Living Bomb or Zeratul’s Singularity Spike). This can be done with Cleanse ability that some Support heroes have:

Self-casted

Hero Class Ability Type
Leoric Warrior Wraith Walk Basic
Johanna Warrior Iron Skin Trait
Samuro Assassin Bladestorm Heroic

Due to the limitations various debuffs place on using abilities, it’s obvious that self-castable Unstoppable cannot remove stuns or silences but can free you from roots or remove blinds and attack/movement slows.

“Unstoppable frames”

Some heroes can either have a very short period of Unstoppable effect on their abilities (like Sylvanas teleporting by reactivating her Haunting Wave ability) or sometimes even “undocumented” Unstoppable effect during ability animation. These brief “frames” can be used to dodge a displacement ability or stun.

A rather simple to reproduce example goes as follows: if Sylvanas who affected by Chromie’s Temporal Loop heroic ability times her Haunting Wave teleport so that she’s between her original and target locations when Temporal Loop teleport effect should’ve taken place, she will not be teleported back to the TL cast place but will arrive to her HW destination point instead. Demonstration: “Wave the Loop” by Fellewin

History lesson: back in the times when Muradin had Unstoppable frames during Dwarf Toss basic ability animation, players was using DT paired with Heavy Impact talent to interrupt enemy E.T.C.’s Mosh Pit.

Protected

Protected status grants affected hero immunity from any damage. This positive status effect is relatively new in the HOTS universe, so as of November 2016 only two heroes have the ability to cast Protected:

Hero Class Ability Type Target
Varian Warrior/Assassin Parry Basic Only self
Medivh Specialist Force of Will Basic Self and allied heroes

Protected effect is great against a hero or group of heroes that are capable of inflicting a lot of damage in a short amount of time (a.k.a. “burst”).

Invulnerable

Invulnerable is the ultimate form of countering negative effects as it makes affected hero to be immune to all damage and all CC and also removes all negative effects (so it’s more than combination of Unstoppable and Protected). Only certain heroes have abilities that grant Invulnerable:

Invulnerable is a very powerful effect that can turn the tide of the game and that’s why in the cases it can be casted on other heroes it is tied to a heroic ability.

Other buffs

As in the same paragraph from “Negative effects”, there exist abilities that are not directly anti-CC.

Increased movement or attack speed

Counterpart to slows and anti-CC in some sense. Speed increase makes it easier to retreat from enemies who chase you or your allies, chase after retreating enemies or boost auto-attack damage-per-second (and some effects that rely on the number of auto-attacks like quest talents or healing)

Shield

While poison was a damage-over-time, shields are like “HP-over-time”. They form an additional level of protection and block some amount of damage that might’ve otherwise reduced damaged hero’s HP.

Shields expire after some time, even when they haven’t blocked any damage. However, Tassadar’s Khala’s Embrace talent allows 50% of shields retain on the target even after expiration.

Just like poison, shields are available to a lot of classes. Also, usually shields in form of basic abilities can be casted on yourself as well as on allied heroes and even structures. Examples:

Hero Class Ability Type Target
Zarya Warrior Personal Barrier / Shield Ally Basic Self / Allied hero
Kerrigan Assassin Assimilation Trait Only self; automatic
Tassadar Support Plasma Shield Basic Self, allied hero or structure
Abathur Specialist Carapace Basic Allied hero under Symbiote
Resistance and Spell Shield

Resistance and Spell Shield are counterparts to Vulnerability. Target affected by Resistance receives less damage from the enemies' auto-attacks while Spell Shield reduces ability damage.

History lesson: Spell Shield used to be a passive ability that mitigated the first portion damage received from any ability. As a quality-of-life change, it was turned into an “on-off” talent so that it can either be always enabled, mimicking the old passive behaviour or toggled just in time to mitigate high-damage spell and not to be wasted on some low-damage threats.

Damage amplification
There exist some abilities that increase damage dealt by affected hero with both auto-attacks and abilities, for example, Tyrael’s level 20 Sanctification upgrade, Holy Arena.
Combination of buffs

As with debuffs, there exist abilities that combine several positive effects. For example, Lt. Morales’ Stim Drone heroic ability grants +75% attack speed and +25% movement speed.
